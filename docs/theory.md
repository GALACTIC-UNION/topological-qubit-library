# Topological Quantum Computing: Theory Overview

## Motivation

Conventional qubits are sensitive to local perturbations — any tiny environmental fluctuation can cause errors. Topological quantum computing encodes information in **global** properties of a physical system that are invariant under local perturbations, providing inherent error protection.

## Anyons

Anyons are quasiparticles in 2D systems with exchange statistics that are neither bosonic nor fermionic. Their **non-abelian** braiding statistics — where exchanging two anyons applies a unitary rotation to the quantum state — is the foundation of topological quantum gates.

## Key Anyon Models

| Model | Universal? | Physical Candidate |
|-------|-----------|--------------------|
| Ising anyons | No (needs π/8 gate) | ν=5/2 FQH, Majorana wires |
| Fibonacci anyons | Yes | ν=12/5 FQH (theoretical) |
| Zₙ parafermions | Partial | Fractional QH systems |
